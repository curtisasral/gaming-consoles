The Nintendo 64, or N64, is Nintendo's third home video game console for the international market. It was named the N64 for its 64-bit processing system, it was released June 1996 in Japan, and September 1996 in North America. It was the last major home game console to use cartridges until the Nintendo Switch (2017).
